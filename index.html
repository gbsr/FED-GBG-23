Welcome to FEX 2023
A conference for Frontend Developers, by Frontend Developers.

Get ready to dig deep into the beautiful world of Frontend Development

In the small, beautiful, but very windy town of Gothenburg, Sweden, we are all about Accessibility this year. For example, since some people have color-blindness, we've decided for this years page to include a ridiculous amount of colors.

This way we can (probably) deliver colors that everyone can appreciate!

We've also included a small white text-shadow on the text to make it more legible. If you squint hard enough, I'm sure you can see it. Don't worry if you don't know what that means. This is we are here:

To help you level up your skills in Frontend Development

Talks for this year is focused on accessibility, responsive design, and the most common layouts.

Of course, we don't care about the most common layouts, because why should we? We decided to go full on late 90's, early 2000 scrap book style instead, because.. why not?

(Breaking the rules is ok)

Nevertheless, the talks we're hosting is all about Better Web™ and wants to make sure that we (or you, as it is) follow both the rules and the most common design principles, so get your ticket now and
conform!

FEX

FEX is a conference for Frontend Developers, meticulously crafted by Frontend Developers

As the creators of FEX, we are very proud of our lineage. It all started as two developers started having Hackathons every other week. We sat in coffee-shops. We coded at bars. We spent time in each other's apartments.

As time went by, more developers wanted to join and suddenly we realized that neither the apartments, the bars, nor the coffee-shops were big enough. Suddenly we had a bi-weekly meet-up and it was growing.

We needed more.

More space.

More challenges.

More people.

As time went by we continued to grow, and so did our requirements. No longer was a bi-weekly hackathon a thing, where people gathered and created something random during 3 days time.

People started to ask if they could hold seminars and talks about their creations. Participants started to join just to join for the talks, and not develop anything, as per the previous Hackathon 'rules'.

This is when we knew we were on to something big

FEX was finally born!

Our first ever real conference was held at Eriksbergs Conference Hall in 2021, and hosted 25 talks and more than 500 visitors!

Talks

Code Like It's 1999: A Nostalgic Journey into Web Development

08:45 AM - 09:45 AM | Cloud Center

Join the charismatic web developer and tech historian Emily Johnson, as she takes us on an exhilarating journey back to the early days of web development. As a seasoned web architect with a passion for the roots of modern coding practices, she will delve into the whimsical world of HTML tables, inline CSS, and the pioneering spirit of the '90s internet era. Attendees can expect to walk away with a newfound appreciation for the evolution of web technologies and a fresh perspective on the trajectory of modern web development.

404 Pages That Make You LOL: A Showcase of Hilarious Errors

10:00 AM - 10:45 AM | Cloud Center

Discover the delightful intersection of accessibility and humor with renowned digital artist and advocate for inclusive design, Mr. Alex Smith, in his engaging talk, "404 Pages That Make You LOL: A Showcase of Hilarious Errors." Mr. Smith, a passionate proponent of user-friendly web experiences, will showcase a collection of the most ingeniously designed and inclusive 404 error pages from around the web. Attendees can expect to gain insights into the art of weaving accessibility into every aspect of digital design, leaving with a newfound understanding of the impact of inclusive humor on user engagement and satisfaction.

The Art of Rapid Prototyping: How to create a mess and worry about it later

11:15 AM - 12:00 PM | Cloud Center

Dive into the thrilling world of rapid prototyping with the fearless innovator and seasoned product designer, Ms. Sarah Adams, in her intriguing talk, "The Art of Rapid Prototyping: How to create a mess and worry about it later." Ms. Adams, known for her trailblazing approaches to product development, will unveil the secrets of agile creation and rapid iteration, highlighting the value of embracing chaos to unlock groundbreaking design solutions. Attendees can anticipate gaining valuable insights into the power of experimentation and risk-taking in the pursuit of revolutionary product innovation, leaving with a newfound appreciation for the art of navigating uncertainty in the creative process.

From Desktop to Pocket: Crafting a Responsive Web Adventure

01:00 PM - 1:45 PM | Cloud Center

Join the visionary digital strategist and user experience expert Michael Chen, as he takes you on an insightful exploration of responsive web design. Chen, a pioneer in adaptive design methodologies, will guide attendees through the dynamic landscape of crafting seamless web experiences across multiple devices. Expect to uncover the intricacies of tailoring user interfaces for diverse screen sizes, leaving with a heightened understanding of the pivotal role of responsive design in shaping the future of digital interaction.

01:00 PM - 1:45 PM | Cloud Center

Web Accessibility: Making the Internet a Friendlier Place

Join the compassionate accessibility advocate and seasoned web developer, Ms. Rachel Thompson, as she sheds light on the importance of inclusive design in her empowering talk, "Web Accessibility: Making the Internet a Friendlier Place." Ms. Thompson, known for her unwavering commitment to creating digital experiences for all users, will delve into the fundamentals of building accessible web interfaces and the positive impact of designing with diverse user needs in mind. Attendees can expect to gain valuable insights into the transformative power of accessibility, leaving with a renewed dedication to championing a more inclusive and welcoming online environment for all.
